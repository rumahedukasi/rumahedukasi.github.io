24. "don't put your book on my table!"the teacher said to rio the teacher told rio???


Answer:

The direction of the question is changing the direct speech to indirect speech. Before explaining the answer, it’s better for us to know about direct speech and indirect speech first.

English has two ways to narrate the spoken-words of a person, they are:

- Direct speech is reporting what someone else said or wrote by using the actual words of the speaker or writer.

Example: Jenny said, “I went to Bali yesterday”

- Indirect speech, which is also knows as reported speech, is reporting what someone else said or wrote but not using the same words as what the person said or wrote.

Example: Jenny said that she had gone to Bali the previous day.

 

How to change direct speech into indirect speech?

There are some rules to do that:

1. Indirect speech doesn’t put the words of the speaker in quotation marks.

2. Indirect always use conjunction “that”. This conjunction is always used between reporting verb and reported speech.

Example: Direct speech: Jenny said, “I went to Bali yesterday”

Indirect speech: Jenny said that she had gone to Bali the previous day.

3. If the reporting verb is either present tense or future tense, the tense of the reported speech will not change. But, if the reporting verb is past tense, the tense of the reported speech will change.

Example: Direct speech: Jenny says, “I went to Bali yesterday”

Indirect speech: Jenny says that she went to Bali the previous day. (there’s no tense changing)

Example: Direct speech: Jenny said, “I went to Bali yesterday”

Indirect speech: Jenny said that she had gone to Bali the previous day. (tense changed)

4. The pronoun will be changed.

Example: Direct speech: Jenny said, “I went to Bali yesterday”

Indirect speech: Jenny said that she had gone to Bali the previous day.

5. If there’s time signal mentioned in the reported speech it will be changed in indirect speech. The example of time changing: now into then, today into that day, yesterday into the previous day, and tomorrow into the next day.

Example: Direct speech: Jenny said, “I went to Bali yesterday”

Indirect speech: Jenny said that she had gone to Bali the previous day.

 

Because the question is in imperative form, I will give how to change direct speech to indirect speech for imperative sentence.

Imperative sentence can express a request, an order, a suggestion, a prohibition, or an advice.

To change imperative direct speech into indirect speech, there are some certain verbs will be put in reported verb instead of said, they are request, order, suggest, forbid, advise or tell.

For example: Direct speech: The boss said to him “close the door!”

Indirect speech: the boss ordered him to close the door.

There are some things you should notice in changing imperative direct speech to indirect speech:

1. You should be able to identify whether the imperative sentence is a request, an order, a suggestion, a prohibition, or an advice.

2. Imperative indirect speech doesn’t use conjunction “that” between reported verb and reported speech. It uses conjunction “to” instead of “that” because imperative sentence means you make someone does something as what you said.

 

So, here is the answer to the question:

Direct speech: "don't put your book on my table!" the teacher said to Rio.

Because the sentence is a prohibition, I will use forbid as the reported verb.

Indirect speech: the teacher forbade Rio to put his book on his/her table.

You also can use tell as the reported verb. It will be:

the teacher told Rio not to put his book on his/her table.
