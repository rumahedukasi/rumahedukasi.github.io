18. majas / Figure of speech lagu How Far I'll Go BY Alessia Cara plss di jawab


Jawaban:

Personification

"See the line where the sky meets the sea? It calls me"​

The line "See where the sky meets the sea" would not have the ability to physically call anyone. This shows Moana's desire to explore beyond her limits and see what the sea has to offer.

Hyperbole

"I've been staring at the edge of the water

'Long as I can remember, never really knowing why"

Moana exaggerates the amount of time she has spent looking at the ocean. It illustrates Moana's desire to visit the sea since she was a child.

Personification  

"And it seems like it's calling out to me, so come find me"

The ocean, Moana says, is calling out to her. The ocean cannot physically communicate with others. She is imbuing the ocean with a characteristic. This attribute is given to the ocean to illustrate how it tempts Moana when she is near it.

Alliteration

"If the wind in my sail on the sea stays behind me."

Since the s sound appears three times in this section, it is an alliteration. It adds to the song's appeal.

Hyperbole

The line refers to the ocean's foreverness. Moana exaggerates the distance by suggesting that no one knows how far it is. But everyone does know how far the ocean stretches. The ocean seems to be endless.

"And no one knows, how far it goes"

Alliteration

Since the t sound appears four times in this line, it is an alliteration. It has a nice ring to it.

"Every turn I take, every trail I track."

Rhetorical Question

"And let me know, what's beyond that line, will I cross that line?"

Moana is asking a question to which she isn't actually looking for an answer. She's simply saying that she intends to move forward.

Parallelism

"See the line where the sky meets the sea? It calls me."

"See the light as it shines on the sea? It's blinding."

Both lines begin with the same words, but the following words shift when you read the line. It emphasizes how appealing the sea is, but Moana changes it by saying that the need to go has become blinding due to how intense it has become.

Symbolism

"The line where the sky meets the sea"

From Moana's perspective, the horizon is the line where the sky meets the sea.

Makna dari original soundtrack film Moana yang berjudul 'How far I'll go' adalah tentang seberapa sejauh sang tokoh 'aku' dalam lagu tersebut akan berusaha mencari jati dirinya demi eksistensi dirinya.

Penjelasan:

Lagu OST Moana berjudul 'How far I'll go' yang dibawakan oleh Alessia Cara ini memiliki makna yang sama dengan tema yang ingin disampaikan dalam film itu sendiri. Makna utama dari lagu ini yang dominan adalah pencarian jati diri seorang Moana, gadis muda yang memiliki passion yang berbeda dari perempuan pada umumnya di daerah asalnya. Bagaimana keinginan dan minatnya berbeda daripada lainnya yaitu kecintaan akan bahari. Di bagian awal lirik lagu tersebut dapat terlihat di kalimat ini

' I wish I could be the perfect daughter

but I come back to the water, no matter how hard I try'

Di bagian kedua usai chorus, hal tersebut kian kentara tersampaikan

' I know everybody on this island, seems so happy on this island

Everything is by design

I know everybody on this island has a role on this island

So maybe I can roll with mine.'

Usaha pencarian jati diri bagi sang tokoh 'aku' dalam lagu yang sempat diganggu oleh rasa bimbang karena berbeda dari yang lain, yang terlihat pada bagian ini

'but the voice inside sings a different song. What is wrong with me?'

Hal itu tidak membuatnya berhenti untuk mengikuti suara hatinya untuk bertualang menuju samudra nun jauh ke tempat yang belum dikenalinya.

Hal tersebut terlihat pada bagian ini

'See the line where the sky meets the sea? It calls me

and no one knows, how far it goes.

If the wind in my sail on the sea stays behind me

One day I'll know, how far I'll go'
