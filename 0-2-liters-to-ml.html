Staying healthy is essential to lead a happy and fulfilling life. There are various things one can do to maintain good health, and these include exercising regularly, eating a balanced and nutritious diet, drinking an adequate amount of water, and getting enough rest.

Exercising is a vital component of staying healthy. Regular exercise helps keep our muscles and bones strong, reduces the risk of chronic diseases, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Exercise can come in many forms, from running or cycling to yoga or swimming. Even small amounts of exercise, like taking a short walk or stretching, can have significant health benefits.

In addition to exercise, it is essential to eat a healthy and balanced diet. This means eating a variety of foods from all food groups, including fruits and vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. It is also essential to limit processed and high-fat foods, sugary drinks, and alcohol. Eating a balanced diet can help maintain a healthy weight, reduce the risk of chronic diseases, and improve overall health and well-being.

Drinking an adequate amount of water is another critical component of staying healthy. The human body is made up of about 60% water, and drinking enough water helps maintain bodily functions like regulating body temperature and removing waste products. Experts recommend drinking at least 2 liters of water every day, and more if you are exercising or in hot weather.

Finally, getting enough rest is crucial for staying healthy. A lack of sleep can affect overall health and well-being, and can lead to a variety of health problems like fatigue, weight gain, and increased risk of chronic diseases. Experts recommend getting at least 7-8 hours of sleep every night to help maintain good health.

In conclusion, staying healthy requires a commitment to regular exercise, a healthy and balanced diet, drinking enough water, and getting enough rest. By making these behaviors part of our daily routine, we can enjoy good health and a happy and fulfilling life.

23. 7. A car requires 4 liters of petrol to travel 108 km. If the car contains 12 liters of petrol, the distance that can be traveled is ... a. 328 km b. 324 km c. 260 km d. 256 km ​


To find the distance that can be traveled with 12 liters of petrol, we can use the concept of proportionality.

The car requires 4 liters of petrol to travel 108 km. We can set up a proportion to find the distance that can be traveled with 12 liters of petrol:

4 liters / 108 km = 12 liters / x km

Cross-multiplying, we get:

4x = 12 * 108
4x = 1296
x = 1296 / 4
x = 324

Therefore, the distance that can be traveled with 12 liters of petrol is 324 km.

So, the correct answer is b. 324 km.

Cindy made some fruit punch for her party. Her friends drank 2 3/4 liters of the fruit punch. Cindy then made another 1 1/2 liters of fruit punch. After that, there was 3 7/8 liters of fruit punch left. How much fruit punch did Cindy make at first?


PENYELESAIAN

Soal di atas jika diterjemahkan ke Bahasa Indonesia adalah sebagai berikut:

Cindy membuat minuman untuk pestanya. Teman-temannya meminum 2 3/4 liter dari minuman itu. Cindy kemudian membuat kembali minuman sebanyak 1 1/2 liter. Setelah itu, terdapat sisa minuman sebanyak 3 7/8 liter. Berapa banyak minuman yang dibuat oleh Cindy mula-mula?


Diketahui:

Banyak minuman yang diminum teman Cindy = 2 3/4 = 11/4 liter

Banyak minuman yang dibuat Cindy kedua kalinya = 1 1/2 = 3/2 liter

Sisa minuman = 3 7/8 = 31/8 liter


Dicari:

Banyak minuman mula-mula = ?


Banyak minuman mula-mula - Banyak minuman yang diminum teman Cindy - Banyak minuman yang dibuat Cindy kedua kalinya = Sisa minuman

Banyak minuman mula-mula - 11/4 + 3/2 = 31/8

Banyak minuman mula-mula = 31/8 + 11/4 - 3/2

Banyak minuman mula-mula = 31/8 + 22/8 - 12/8

Banyak minuman mula-mula = 41/8 = 5 1/8 liter

Jadi, banyak minuman yang dibuat Cindy mula-mula adalah 5 1/8 liter.

To find out how many liters are needed to make it full, we need to calculate the remaining volume. Since 1 dm³ is equal to 1 liter, the remaining volume needed to fill the tub can be calculated as:
