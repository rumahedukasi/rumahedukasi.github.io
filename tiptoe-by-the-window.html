1. The men sing a new song -› A new song is sung by the men.

2. The men are singing a new song -› A new song is being sung by the men.

3. The men have sung a new song -› A new song has been sung by the men.

4. The men sang a new song -› A new song was sung by the men.

5. The men were singing a new song -› A new song was being sung by the men.

6. The men had sung a new song -› A new song had been sung by the men.

7. The men will sing a new song -› A new song will be sung by the men.

Penjelasan:

Passive Voice

Simple Present

Active: S + V¹ + O

Passive: S + am/are/is + V³ + O

Present Continuous

Active: S + am/are/is + V-ing + O

Passive: S + am/are/is + being + V³ + O

Present Perfect

Active: S + has/have + V³ + O

Passive: S + has/have + been + V³ + O

Simple Past

Active: S + V² + O

Passive: S + was/were + V³ + O

Past Continuous

Active: S + was/were + V-ing + O

Passive: S + was/were + being + V³ + O

Past Perfect

Active: S + had + V³ + O

Passive: S + had + been + V³ + O

Simple Future

Active: S + will + V¹ + O

Passive: S + will + be + V³ + O

10. While mrs.rina (read) her little boy a strory.he (fall) asleep.so she(close) the book and quietly (tiptoe) out of the room


while Mrs. Rina read her little boy a story, he fell asleep. So she closed the book and quietly tiptoed out of the room

Subject : English

Category : Tenses (Past Simple and Past Continuous)

Level : JHS VIII

Keywords : when, while

Code : 5

Past Simple Tense is used to describe an event that happened and completed in the past.

Pattern : S + Preterite (v₂)

The past continuous tense is used to describe an event that was happening round a specific time in the past.

Pattern : S + was/were + present participle

Past continuous and past simple are often used together. The past continuous describes the longer or background action while the past simple describes the action that interrupts the background action.

ANSWERS :

While Mrs Rina WAS READING her little boy a strory, he FELL asleep so she CLOSED the book and quietly TIPTOED out of the room.
